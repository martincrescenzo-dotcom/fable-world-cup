## A4. The reward environment — **CORRECTED MODEL (user clarification 2026-06-20)**
**Rewards are FIXED and pre-set by the game-maker; crowd size / field split does NOT change reward level.**
Only the **exact-score rarity BONUS is dynamic** (tiered by the share of correct-outcome pickers who chose
that exact score: >30%→+20, 20–30%→+30, 5–20%→+50, 0.5–5%→+70, <0.5%→+100).

2. **This OVERTURNS the old "fair-game: winner-picking earns ~0" doctrine.** That claim assumed

(always-large: even 1% ≈ tens of thousands) pool, so it is independent of how popular the outcome was. The
bonus is driven by SCORE-level salience herding (the crowd_params model), a separate quantity from outcome
field%. Pick selection is unchanged (EV = blend_p × fixed_reward, argmax — always was), but
the *edge is now measured cleanly* as sharp-market vs the game-maker's fixed line, decoupled from the crowd.

  **mandatory** every matchday — it is the load-bearing layer.

  picks) = **DECORRELATE** onto field-underpicked + market-confirmed +EV outcomes, paying ≤5% EV.

  retired (backtest showed no realised edge; crowd model unconverged).
